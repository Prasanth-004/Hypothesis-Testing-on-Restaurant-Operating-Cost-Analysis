# Hypothesis Testing on Restaurant Operating Cost Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project demonstrates the application of **Hypothesis Testing** to determine whether the average weekly operating cost of restaurant franchises differs significantly from the theoretical operating cost predicted by a mathematical model. Statistical testing techniques are used to make data-driven decisions based on sample observations.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Understand the fundamentals of hypothesis testing.
* Formulate null and alternative hypotheses.
* Calculate the theoretical operating cost.
* Compute the test statistic using a Z-test.
* Determine the p-value and critical value.
* Make statistical decisions based on the significance level.
* Interpret the results in a business context.

---

## 📂 Problem Statement

Bombay Hospitality Ltd. operates restaurant franchises and estimates weekly operating costs using the following theoretical model:

**W = 1000 + 5X**

where:

* **W** = Weekly Operating Cost
* **X** = Number of units produced

A sample of restaurant operating costs is analyzed to determine whether the observed average cost significantly differs from the theoretical expectation.

---

## 🔄 Project Workflow

### 1. Problem Understanding

* Studied the business scenario.
* Identified the theoretical operating cost model.

### 2. Hypothesis Formulation

Defined:

* Null Hypothesis (H₀)
* Alternative Hypothesis (H₁)

to evaluate the population mean.

### 3. Test Statistic Calculation

* Calculated the theoretical mean.
* Computed the standard error.
* Calculated the Z-test statistic.

### 4. Statistical Testing

* Selected the significance level (α = 0.05).
* Determined the critical value.
* Calculated the p-value.

### 5. Decision Making

Compared:

* Test Statistic
* Critical Value
* P-value

to decide whether to reject or fail to reject the null hypothesis.

### 6. Interpretation

Provided a statistical interpretation of the results and explained the business implications.
